Non-AC ambulances are the standard type of ambulances that do not have air conditioning systems. While they lack the comfort provided by air conditioning, they still play a crucial role in providing emergency medical services and transporting patients to healthcare facilities. Here are some details about non-AC ambulances:

  1. Medical Equipment: Like AC ambulances, non-AC ambulances are equipped with essential medical equipment and supplies to provide initial treatment and stabilize patients during transportation. This equipment may include cardiac monitors, defibrillators, oxygen cylinders, stretchers, medical kits, and other necessary supplies.
  2. Communication Systems: Non-AC ambulances are equipped with communication systems to ensure effective communication between medical staff inside the ambulance and healthcare facilities. These communication systems can include radios, mobile phones, or two-way communication devices.
  3. Safety Features: Safety features such as seat belts, stretcher restraints, and emergency lighting are essential components of non-AC ambulances to ensure the safety of patients and medical personnel during transportation.
  4. Ventilation: While non-AC ambulances lack air conditioning, they typically have ventilation systems to ensure adequate airflow within the vehicle. This helps maintain a comfortable environment and prevent overheating, especially during hot weather.
  5. Cost-effectiveness: Non-AC ambulances are often more affordable to purchase and maintain compared to their AC counterparts. This makes them a practical choice for healthcare providers operating in regions with milder climates or limited budgets.

Overall, while non-AC ambulances may lack the comfort provided by air conditioning, they are still equipped to provide essential emergency medical services and transportation for patients in need.
